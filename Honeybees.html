Honeybees

Honeybees are important pollinators for flowers, fruits, and vegetables. They live on stored honey and pollen all winter and cluster into a ball to conserve warmth.

Honeybees are eusocial flying insects belonging to the genus Apis. They are native to mainland Afro-Eurasia and are known for their ability to produce honey.

What do honeybees eat?

Honeybees collect nectar and pollen from flowers. Both are stored in the hive where nectar is converted to honey, and pollen is fermented into bee bread. Bees eat honey and bee bread.

Where do honeybees live?

Honey bees can thrive in natural or domesticated environments, though they prefer to live in gardens, woodlands, orchards, meadows and other areas where flowering plants are abundant.
